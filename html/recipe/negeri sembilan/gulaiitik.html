Gulai Itik

Gulai Itik is a traditional Malaysian dish originating from Negeri Sembilan, renowned for its rich and aromatic flavors. The name "Gulai" refers to a type of curry, while "Itik" means duck in Malay. This dish is a celebration of the Minangkabau influence in the region, combining local spices and coconut milk to create a hearty and flavorful curry.

Cook time

1 hour

Pax

2 pax

Tags: spicy meat

Instructions

Step 1

Clean the duck pieces and pat them dry with paper towels. Set aside.

Step 2

Heat oil in a large pot or wok over medium heat.

Step 3

Add the blended spice paste (dried red chilies, shallots, garlic, ginger, turmeric) and saut√© until fragrant and the oil starts to separate from the paste.

Step 4

Add the duck pieces to the pot and cook until the duck is browned on all sides.

Step 5

Pour in the coconut milk and water. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and turmeric leaves.

Step 6

Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 45 minutes, or until the duck is tender and the sauce has thickened.

Step 7

Add tamarind peel if using, and season with salt. Adjust sweetness with sugar if desired.

Step 8

Simmer for a few more minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Step 9

Serve hot with steamed rice or traditional Malaysian rice cakes (ketupat).

Ingredients

1 whole duck (about 1.5 kg, cut into pieces), 2 tablespoons oil, spice paste (5-6 dried red chilies (soaked and blended), 4 shallots (blended), 3 cloves garlic (blended), 1 thumb-sized ginger (blended), 1 thumb-sized turmeric (blended), 2 stalks lemongrass (smashed), 2-3 kaffir lime leaves (torn into pieces), curry (400 ml coconut milk, 1 cup water, 2-3 turmeric leaves (torn into pieces), 1-2 pieces of tamarind peel (optional, for tanginess), salt to taste, 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, to balance flavors)
