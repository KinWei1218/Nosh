Rendang Minang

Rendang Minang is a classic Indonesian dish with deep roots in Negeri Sembilan's cuisine, thanks to the influence of the Minangkabau people. Originating from West Sumatra, this dish is renowned for its rich, spicy, and aromatic flavors. Traditionally, itâ€™s served during special occasions and gatherings.

Cook time

3 hours

Pax

4 pax

Tags: spicy meat

Instructions

Step 1

Heat oil in a pot and fry the blended spice paste until fragrant.

Step 2

Add beef cubes and stir-fry until the meat is browned.

Step 3

Pour in coconut milk and water, then add lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and turmeric leaves.

Step 4

Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the beef is tender and the sauce thickens.

Step 5

Stir in tamarind paste and season with salt.

Step 6

Serve with rice or ketupat.

Ingredients

500g beef (cut into cubes), 200ml coconut milk, 1 cup water, 2 tablespoons oil, 2 stalks lemongrass (bruised), 4-5 kaffir lime leaves, 2 turmeric leaves (torn into pieces), 1 tablespoon tamarind paste, salt to taste, blended spice paste (5-6 dried red chilies (soaked and blended), 4 shallots, 3 cloves garlic, 1 thumb-sized ginger, 1 thumb-sized turmeric
