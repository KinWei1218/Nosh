Cucur Badak

Cucur Badak is a popular Malaysian snack that originates from Negeri Sembilan. It is a type of deep-fried pastry made from a dough of rice flour and all-purpose flour, filled with a sweet and spicy coconut mixture. The name "Cucur Badak" translates roughly to "badak fritters," with "badak" meaning "rhinoceros" in Malay, which refers to the shape of the fritters.

Cook time

1 hour

Pax

4 pax

Tags: spicy

Instructions

Step 1

Mix rice flour, all-purpose flour, grated coconut, and salt in a bowl. Gradually add water to form a smooth dough.

Step 2

For the filling, mix grated coconut, palm sugar, and white sugar in a pan and cook until the sugar dissolves and the mixture thicken.

Step 3

Take small pieces of dough, flatten them, and place a spoonful of filling in the center. Seal the edges and shape into small balls.

Step 4

Heat oil in a pan and fry the balls until golden brown.

Step 5

Drain on paper towels and serve.

Ingredients

1 cup rice flour, 1 cup all-purpose flour, 1 cup grated coconut, 200g sweet potatoes (boiled and mashed), 1 tablespoon salt, oil for frying, filling (200g grated coconut, 100g palm sugar (shredded), 1 tablespoon white sugar)
