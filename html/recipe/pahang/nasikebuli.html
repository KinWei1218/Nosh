Nasi Kebuli

Nasi Kebuli, also known as Nasi Kabuli, is a traditional rice dish with Middle Eastern influences, popular in Pahang, Malaysia. It features aromatic basmati rice cooked with a rich blend of spices, coconut milk, and meat, often served during special occasions or festive gatherings.

Cook time

1 hour

Pax

1 pax

Tags: spicy rice

Instructions

Step 1

Heat oil in a pot and saut√© the onion, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.

Step 2

Add the cinnamon stick, cloves, and star anise. Stir for a minute.

Step 3

Add the chicken or beef and cook until browned.

Step 4

Pour in the coconut milk and add the ground spices (coriander, cumin, fennel).

Step 5

Add the washed rice and stir to coat the rice with the spices.

Step 6

Add water to cover the rice, season with salt and sugar, and bring to a boil.

Step 7

Reduce the heat, cover the pot, and let it simmer until the rice is cooked and fluffy.

Step 8

Serve with a side of pickles or sambal.

Ingredients

2 cups basmati rice (washed and drained), 500g chicken or beef (cut into pieces), 2 cups coconut milk, 1 onion (sliced), 2 cloves garlic (chopped), 1-inch ginger (chopped), 1 cinnamon stick, 3 cloves, 2 star anise, 1 teaspoon ground coriander, 1 teaspoon ground cumin, 1 teaspoon ground fennel, salt and sugar to taste, cooking oil
