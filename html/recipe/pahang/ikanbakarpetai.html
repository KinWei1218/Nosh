Ikan Bakar Petai

Ikan Bakar Petai is a flavorful and aromatic dish that combines the smoky taste of grilled fish with the pungent, slightly bitter flavor of petai (stink beans). The fish, often a whole mackerel or tilapia, is marinated with a spicy, tangy blend of chili paste, garlic, and tamarind, then wrapped in banana leaves and grilled over an open flame. The result is a tender, juicy fish with a complex flavor profile, balanced by the unique taste of the petai.

Cook time

1 hours

Pax

2 pax

Tags: seafood

Instructions

Step 1

Mix chili paste, garlic, onion, and tamarind paste to make a marinade.

Step 2

Marinate the fish with the mixture and stuff with petai.

Step 3

Wrap the fish in banana leaves and grill until cooked through.

Step 4

Serve with rice and sambal.

Ingredients

1 whole fish (e.g., mackerel or tilapia) 2 tbsp chili paste 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 onion, chopped 1 tbsp tamarind paste 1 cup petai (stink beans) Banana leaves for wrapping
