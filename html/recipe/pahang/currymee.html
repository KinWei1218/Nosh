Curry Mee

Curry Mee is a beloved Malaysian noodle dish, known for its rich, spicy coconut curry broth. The dish typically features yellow egg noodles or rice vermicelli, served with a variety of toppings like shrimp, chicken, tofu puffs, and boiled eggs. The broth is made with a blend of curry spices, coconut milk, and sometimes dried shrimp, giving it a deep, complex flavor.

Cook time

50 minutes

Pax

2 pax

Tags: seafood

Instructions

Step 1

Cook the noodles according to package instructions.

Step 2

Stir-fry the chicken or shrimp with curry powder, onion, and garlic.

Step 3

Add coconut milk and simmer to create a thick broth.

Step 4

Serve the broth over the noodles, garnished with bean sprouts and boiled egg.

Ingredients

200g yellow noodles 200g chicken or shrimp 2 tbsp curry powder 1 cup coconut milk 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced Bean sprouts and boiled egg for garnish
