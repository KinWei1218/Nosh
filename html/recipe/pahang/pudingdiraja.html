Puding Diraja

Puding Diraja, or Royal Pudding, is a traditional Malaysian dessert that hails from the state of Pahang. This dessert is renowned for its elegant presentation and luxurious flavor profile. Traditionally, it was served to royalty, hence the name "Diraja," which means "royal" in Malay. The pudding features a delightful combination of bananas, prunes, and cashew nuts, all enveloped in a rich and creamy egg custard.

Cook time

2 hours

Pax

2 pax

Tags: desserts

Instructions

Step 1

Arrange the banana slices, prunes, and cashew nuts in a serving dish.

Step 2

Mix the fish paste with sago flour, rice flour, salt, and sugar. Knead until the dough is smooth.

Step 3

In a bowl, beat the eggs with sugar until light and fluffy.

Step 4

Pour the egg mixture over the arranged bananas, prunes, and cashew nuts.

Step 5

Steam the pudding for 30 minutes or until the custard is set.

Step 6

Allow it to cool before serving.

Ingredients

4 ripe bananas (sliced), 50g prunes, 50g cashew nuts, toasted, 4 eggs, 2 cups evaporated milk, 1 cup sugar, 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
