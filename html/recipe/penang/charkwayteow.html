Char Kway Teow

Char kway teow, Malaysia’s most famous street food, is a simple rice noodle dish made with soy sauce, eggs, cockles, bean sprouts, Chinese sausage and a couple of shrimp. It’s common throughout the country – devoured at roadside stalls or feasted on at hawker centres – but there is only one “king” of char kway teow, and he’s in Penang.

Cook time

20 minutes

Pax

1 pax

Tags: spicy noodles

Instructions

Step 1

Soak noodles in warm water to soften. Beat eggs lightly.

Step 2

Heat lard/oil in a wok. Add garlic and lap cheong, stir-fry until fragrant.

Step 3

Stir-fry prawns until just cooked. Add cockles if using.

Step 4

Add noodles, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and chili paste. Stir-fry to coat evenly.

Step 5

Push the noodles to one side, pour the beaten eggs on the other side, and scramble lightly. Add bean sprouts and chives, and stir-fry everything together.

Step 6

Serve hot.

Ingredients

200g flat rice noodles (kuey teow), 100g prawns (shelled and deveined), 50g cockles (optional), 2 eggs, 100g bean sprouts, 2 tablespoons soy sauce, 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce, 1 tablespoon chili paste, 2 cloves garlic (chopped), 50g Chinese chives (cut into 4cm lengths), 50g lap cheong (Chinese sausage, sliced thinly), 2 tablespoons lard or vegetable oil
