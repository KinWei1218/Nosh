Penang Hokkien Mee

Penang Hokkien mee or Hae Mee, as known to the locals, is a soupy yellow egg noodle dish with prawns. Popular in Penang, Malaysia, the broth of these noodles has a rich prawn flavour. Street hawkers sell this soup noodle dish from morning till usually in the evenings. Accompanied with boiled egg, kangkung, bean spouts, fried shallots and the special sambal, each bite delivers an orchestra of flavours.

Cook time

30 minutes

Pax

1 pax

Tags: spicy noodles

Instructions

Step 1

Boil water and add pork ribs. Simmer for 1 hour. Blend dried prawns and garlic into a paste and fry until fragrant. Add chili paste and fry for another minute.

Step 2

Add prawns to the broth and cook until just done. Remove the prawns and set aside.

Step 3

Cook yellow noodles and rice vermicelli in boiling water and drain.

Step 4

Place noodles in a bowl, pour the broth over, and top with prawns, boiled eggs, and garnishes. Serve with lime wedges.

Ingredients

300g yellow noodles, 200g rice vermicelli (bihun), 300g prawns (shelled and deveined), 300g pork ribs, 2 liters water, 100g dried prawns (soaked), 4 cloves garlic, 5 tablespoons chili paste, 2 tablespoons soy sauce, 100g bean sprouts, 2 boiled eggs (sliced), garnishes (fried shallots, chopped spring onions, and lime wedges)
