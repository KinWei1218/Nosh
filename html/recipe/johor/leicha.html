Lei Cha

Lei Cha is a traditional Hakka dish known for its nourishing and detoxifying properties. The dish is a vibrant mix of textures and flavors, featuring a base of steamed rice topped with a colorful assortment of finely chopped vegetables, nuts, and sometimes tofu. The highlight is the "Lei Cha" or "Thunder Tea," a green, herbal broth made from a blend of fresh herbs like basil and mint, crushed peanuts, sesame seeds, and sometimes green tea.

Cook time

50 minutes

Pax

1 pax

Tags: rice

Instructions

Step 1

Cook the rice as per usual.

Step 2

Stir-fry the chopped vegetables separately until tender.

Step 3

Prepare the lei cha soup by grinding basil, mint, peanuts, sesame seeds, and dried shrimp together with some water until smooth.

Step 4

Pour the lei cha soup over rice and serve with stir-fried vegetables.

Ingredients

cup rice 1/2 cup basil leaves 1/2 cup mint leaves 1/4 cup peanuts (roasted) 1/4 cup sesame seeds (roasted) 2 cups assorted vegetables (chopped, e.g., cabbage, green beans, chives) 1/4 cup dried shrimp Salt to taste
