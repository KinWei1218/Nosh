Mee Bandung Muar

Mee bandung is a Malay noodle dish that originated in Muar. The dish combines yellow noodles with a thick, spicy sauce that consists of chilies, onions, shrimp paste, and dried shrimps. Each mee bandung is topped with a variety of condiments that typically include halved boiled eggs, shrimps, fishcakes, vegetables, and occasionally beef, while the dish is best finished off with a splash of lime juice.

Cook time

30 minutes

Pax

1 pax

Tags: spicy noodles

Instructions

Step 1

Heat oil in a pot and saut√© the garlic, shallots, and chili paste until fragrant.

Step 2

Add the beef slices and cook until they start to brown.

Step 3

Stir in the prawns and dried shrimp. Cook until the prawns turn pink.

Step 4

Add the soy sauce and broth. Bring to a boil.

Step 5

Lower the heat and add the cornflour mixture to thicken the gravy.

Step 6

Slowly pour in the beaten eggs while stirring to create egg strands.

Step 7

Cook the yellow noodles according to package instructions.

Step 8

Serve the noodles topped with the gravy and garnishes.

Ingredients

500g yellow noodles, 200g beef slices, 200g peeled and deveined prawns, 3 tablespoons chili paste, 4 cloves garlic (chopped), 2 shallots (chopped), 2 tablespoons dried shrimp (soaked and blended), 3 tablespoons soy sauce, 2 lightly beaten eggs, 4 cups beef or chicken broth, 1 tablespoon cornflour mixed with 2 tablespoons of water, garnishes (fried shallots, boiled eggs, chopped spring onions, and sliced chilies)
