Johor Laksa

Laksa Johor as the name suggests originates from the southern state of Peninsular Malaysia – Johor. Laksa Johor is unique compared to other laksa as the thick gravy itself is made from creamy coconut and fish base compared to Assam Penang Laksa for with its tangy tamarind soup base, or Laksa Sarawak with its chicken & shrimp broth spiced base. Another distinct feature of Laksa Johor is the noodles itself.

Cook time

20 minutes

Pax

1 pax

Tags: spicy noodles

Instructions

Step 1

Boil the fish in water until cooked. Remove the fish, debone, and flake the flesh. Reserve the fish stock.

Step 2

Blend the fish with some of the reserved stock to form a smooth paste.

Step 3

Heat some oil in a pot and sauté the shallots, garlic, ginger, galangal, lemongrass, and chili paste until fragrant.

Step 4

Add the curry powder, ground coriander, cumin, fennel, and dried shrimp. Continue to cook until the oil separates.

Step 5

Stir in the fish paste, coconut milk, and tamarind paste. Add salt and sugar to taste.

Step 6

Let the gravy simmer for about 30 minutes until it thickens.

Step 7

Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions.

Step 8

Serve the spaghetti topped with the gravy and garnishes.

Ingredients

500g spaghetti (cooked), 500g mackerel fish (boiled and flaked), 400ml coconut milk, 4 tbsp dried shrimp, soaked and blended, 4 tablespoons chili paste, 2 tablespoons curry powder, 1 tablespoon ground coriander, 1 tablespoon ground cumin, 1 tablespoon ground fennel, 4 shallots (chopped), 3 cloves garlic (chopped), 3cm ginger (chopped), 3cm galangal (chopped), 2 stalks lemongrass (bruised), amarind paste (from 2 tbsp tamarind mixed with 1 cup water), salt and sugar to taste, garnishes (shredded cucumber, bean sprouts, daun kesum (Vietnamese coriander), lime wedges, and sambal belacan)
