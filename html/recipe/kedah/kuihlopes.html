Kuih Lopes

Kuih Lopes is a traditional Malay dessert that is especially popular during festive occasions in Kedah. It is made from glutinous rice, wrapped in banana leaves, and steamed before being coated with grated coconut. The triangular shape of Kuih Lopes, its vibrant green hue from pandan, and the contrast of the white grated coconut make it visually appealing and a favorite among locals.

Cook time

1 hour

Pax

2 pax

Tags: desserts

Instructions

Step 1

Drain the soaked glutinous rice and mix it with pandan paste and salt.

Step 2

Cut banana leaves into squares, place a spoonful of rice in the center, and fold into a triangle shape. Secure with a toothpick.

Step 3

Steam the wrapped rice for about 20 minutes until fully cooked.

Step 4

In a small pot, melt the palm sugar with water until it forms a thick syrup.

Step 5

Unwrap the cooked rice, coat it with grated coconut, and serve with the palm sugar syrup.

Ingredients

2 cups glutinous rice (soaked overnight), 1 teaspoon pandan paste (optional), 1/2 teaspoon salt, banana leaves for wrapping, 1 cup grated coconut (steamed with a pinch of salt), 1 cup palm sugar (gula Melaka), 1/2 cup water
