Kuih Karas

Kuih Karas is a traditional Malaysian snack, particularly popular in Kedah. Itâ€™s a delicate, crispy treat made from rice flour and sugar, formed into a lacy, net-like structure. This kuih is often enjoyed during festive occasions or as a light snack with tea. Its simplicity in ingredients belies the intricate, almost artistic process of making it, which results in a visually appealing and tasty treat.

Cook time

30 minutes

Pax

2 pax

Tags: desserts

Instructions

Step 1

Dissolve sugar in water in a mixing bowl.

Step 2

Gradually add the rice flour, mixing until you get a smooth batter.

Step 3

Heat oil in a deep frying pan.

Step 4

Pour the batter into a mold or squeeze bottle, and drizzle it into the hot oil in circular motions to create a lacy pattern.

Step 5

Fry until golden brown, then drain on paper towels.

Step 6

Let it cool before serving.

Ingredients

1 cup rice flour, 1 cup sugar, 1 cup water, oil for frying
