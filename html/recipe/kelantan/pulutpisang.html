Pulut Pisang

Pulut Pisang is a traditional Malaysian dessert popular in Kelantan. This sweet delicacy features glutinous rice steamed with ripe bananas and infused with rich coconut milk and palm sugar. Wrapped in banana leaves, it is both a visual and flavorful treat that highlights the simplicity and depth of Malaysian culinary traditions.

Cook time

1 hour

Pax

3 pax

Tags: Desserts

Instructions

Step 1

Soak the glutinous rice in water for at least 3 hours, then drain.

Step 2

Mix coconut milk, palm sugar, and salt. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.

Step 3

Place a layer of glutinous rice on a banana leaf, add slices of banana on top, and then cover with more rice.

Step 4

Fold the banana leaf over the mixture and steam for about 30 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked.

Ingredients

2 cups of glutinous rice, 2 ripe bananas (peeled and sliced), 1 cup of coconut milk, 1 cup of palm sugar (grated), a pinch of salt, banana leaves for wrapping
