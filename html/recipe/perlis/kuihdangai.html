Kuih Dangai

Kuih Dangai is a traditional Malaysian snack, especially popular in the northern states like Perlis. It is a coconut-based cake made primarily from glutinous rice flour and grated coconut, sweetened with sugar and a pinch of salt. This kuih is often grilled or baked in small molds, giving it a crispy outer layer and a soft, chewy interior.

Cook time

35 minutes

Pax

2 pax

Tags: desserts breakfast

Instructions

Step 1

Set up a steamer and bring the water to a boil. You can line the steamer basket with banana leaves or parchment paper to prevent sticking.

Step 2

In a large bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour, grated coconut, sugar, and salt. Mix well.

Step 3

Gradually add water to the dry ingredients while stirring until you get a thick, smooth batter. The mixture should be thick enough to hold its shape but still pourable.

Step 4

Pour the batter into a lightly greased or lined steaming tray or mold. Smooth the surface with a spatula or the back of a spoon.

Step 5

Steam the batter over high heat for about 30-40 minutes, or until it is cooked through. The kuih should be firm to the touch and not sticky.

Step 6

Allow the kuih to cool completely in the tray before removing it. Once cooled, cut it into squares or slices.

Step 7

If desired, roll the kuih slices in additional grated coconut for extra texture and flavor.

Step 8

Kuih Dangai can be served as a sweet snack or dessert. Enjoy it with tea or coffee.

Ingredients

2 cups glutinous rice flour, 1 cup grated coconut (fresh or desiccated), 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste), 1/2 teaspoon salt, 1 cup water, grated coconut for topping (optional)
