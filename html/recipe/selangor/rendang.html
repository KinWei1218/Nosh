Rendang

Rendang is a traditional Minangkabau dish from West Sumatra, Indonesia, which has become a popular and iconic dish in Malaysia. It's a rich, flavorful beef stew slow-cooked in a mixture of coconut milk and an aromatic blend of spices. Often served during special occasions, Rendang is known for its deep, complex flavors and tender, succulent meat.

Cook time

2 hours

Pax

4 pax

Tags: Spicy Meat

Instructions

Step 1

Blend all spice paste ingredients until smooth.

Step 2

Heat oil in a pot, add spice paste and saut√© until fragrant.

Step 3

Add beef cubes and cook until browned. Add coconut milk, tamarind paste, sugar, lemongrass, and kaffir lime leaves. Simmer over low heat until beef is tender and the sauce is thickened.

Step 4

Serve rendang with rice or roti.

Ingredients

500g cubes beef, 2 cups coconut milk, 2 tablespoons tamarind paste, 1 tablespoon sugar, 2 stalks lemongrass (bruised), 2 kaffir lime leaves, 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, spice paste (5 dried chilies (soaked and blended), 3 cloves garlic, 1 small onion, 1 tablespoon ginger (minced), 1 tablespoon turmeric powder, 1 tablespoon coriander powder, salt to taste)
