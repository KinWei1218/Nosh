Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is a quintessential Malaysian dish often considered the country's national dish. It consists of rice cooked in creamy coconut milk, paired with a variety of traditional accompaniments. It is typically served with sambal (a spicy chili paste), crispy anchovies, roasted peanuts, sliced cucumber, and hard-boiled or fried eggs. Often, additional side dishes like rendang or fried chicken may accompany it.

Cook time

40 minutes

Pax

1 pax

Tags: Spicy Rice Breakfast

Instructions

Step 1

Rinse rice and drain. In a rice cooker, add rice, coconut milk, water, pandan leaf, and a pinch of salt. Cook as per the rice cooker's instructions.

Step 2

For the sambal, heat oil in a pan. Saut√© garlic, onion, and belacan until fragrant.

Step 3

Add blended chilies and cook until the oil separates. Add tamarind paste, sugar, and salt. Simmer until thickened.

Step 4

Serve the rice with sambal, crispy anchovies, peanuts, hard-boiled eggs, and cucumber.

Ingredients

2 cups rice, 2 cups coconut milk, 1 cup water, 1 pandan leaf (knotted), salt to taste, sambal (10 dried chilies (soaked and blended), 3 cloves garlic, 1 small onion, 1 tablespoon belacan (shrimp paste), 2 tablespoons tamarind paste, 1 tablespoon sugar, salt to taste, 2 tablespoons oil), garnish (fried crispy anchovies (ikan bilis), roasted peanuts, hard-boiled eggs, sliced cucumber, sambal)
