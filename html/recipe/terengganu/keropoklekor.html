Keropok Lekor

Keropok Lekor is a quintessential snack from Terengganu, Malaysia. It is a type of fish sausage made from a blend of fish meat, sago flour, and seasonings. The mixture is shaped into long, cylindrical rolls, which are then boiled and typically deep-fried until crispy on the outside. Keropok Lekor is enjoyed by locals and visitors alike and is often served with a spicy, tangy chili sauce.

Cook time

45 minutes

Pax

2 pax

Tags: breakfast

Instructions

Step 1

Blend the fish fillet into a paste.

Step 2

Mix the fish paste with sago flour, rice flour, salt, and sugar. Knead until the dough is smooth.

Step 3

Divide the dough into small portions. Roll each portion into a long cylindrical shape.

Step 4

Bring a pot of water to a boil. Boil the shaped dough until they float. Remove and let them cool.

Step 5

Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the keropok until golden brown.

Step 6

Serve hot with chili sauce.

Ingredients

500g fish fillet (mackerel or sardines), 1 cup sago flour, 1/2 cup rice flour, 1 teaspoon salt, 1/2 teaspoon sugar, oil for frying
