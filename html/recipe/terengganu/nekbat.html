Nekbat

Nekbat is a traditional dessert from Kelantan, Malaysia, made from rice flour and shaped into small, cake-like molds. After steaming, the Nekbat cakes are soaked in a syrup made from sugar, coconut milk, and pandan leaves, giving them a sweet, fragrant flavor. Nekbat is often enjoyed during special occasions, particularly during the fasting month of Ramadan.

Cook time

40 minutes

Pax

6 pax

Tags: desserts

Instructions

Step 1

Mix rice flour with water to form a batter.

Step 2

Pour the batter into molds and steam until set.

Step 3

Boil sugar with coconut milk and pandan juice to make a syrup.

Step 4

Serve the steamed nekbat with the syrup.

Ingredients

2 cups rice flour 1 cup water 1/2 cup sugar 1/2 cup coconut milk 1/4 cup pandan juice
