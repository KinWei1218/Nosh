Nasi Dagang

Nasi Dagang is a traditional dish from the east coast of Peninsular Malaysia, particularly Terengganu. The dish consists of steamed glutinous rice cooked with coconut milk and fenugreek seeds, giving it a rich and aromatic flavor. Nasi Dagang is typically served with a side of tuna curry (Gulai Ikan Tongkol), pickled vegetables, and hard-boiled eggs.

Cook time

1.5 hours

Pax

1 pax

Tags: spicy rice

Instructions

Step 1

Drain the soaked glutinous rice. Steam the rice for about 20 minutes. Mix the coconut milk with salt and pour over the rice.

Step 2

Add fenugreek seeds and pandan leaf. Steam for another 20 minutes until the rice is fully cooked.

Step 3

Heat oil in a pan. Saut√© garlic and onion until fragrant.

Step 4

Add curry powder and chili paste, stirring until the oil separates.

Step 5

Add tuna fillet, tamarind juice, sugar, and salt. Cook until the tuna is tender and the sauce thickens.

Step 6

Serve the steamed glutinous rice with the tuna curry on the side.

Ingredients

2 cups glutinous rice, soaked for 6 hours, 1 cup coconut milk, 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds, 1 pandan leaf, knotted, 500g tuna fillet, 3 tablespoons oil, 2 cloves garlic (minced), 1 onion (sliced), 3 tablespoons curry powder, 1 tablespoon chili paste, 1/2 cup tamarind juice, 1 tablespoon sugar, salt to taste
