Laksam Terengganu

Laksam is a traditional rice noodle dish originating from the east coast state of Terengganu, Malaysia. This dish is distinctively different from other Malaysian noodle dishes due to its thick, flat rice noodles, which are served with a rich, creamy fish-based gravy. The dish is often garnished with fresh herbs and julienned vegetables, offering a delightful combination of flavors and textures.

Cook time

1.5 hours

Pax

1 pax

Tags: noodles

Instructions

Step 1

Mix rice flour, wheat flour, and water to form a smooth batter.

Step 2

Pour a thin layer of batter into a heated non-stick pan and steam for 5 minutes.

Step 3

Roll the steamed batter into a log and slice into thin noodles.

Step 4

Blend the flaked fish with coconut milk, chili paste, onion, garlic, and salt.

Step 5

Simmer the mixture over low heat until it thickens.

Step 6

Serve the noodles topped with the fish gravy, cucumber, and fresh herbs.

Ingredients

2 cups rice flour, 1 cup wheat flour, 2 cups water, 500g mackerel (boiled and flaked), 1 cup coconut milk, 1 tablespoon chili paste, 1 onion (minced), 2 cloves garlic (minced), salt to taste, cucumber (julienned), fresh herbs (mint, basil)
