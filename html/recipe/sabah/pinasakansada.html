Pinasakan Sada

Pinasakan is a popular preserved dish among the Kadazandusun tribe in Sabah. The full name is actually Pinasakan Sada. Literally pinasakan means ‘reducing the volume of liquid through boiling/evaporating’, and sada means ‘fish’. It is also called Parasakan or Pinarasakan, depending on the region, but Pinasakan is a more well known name.

Cook time

45 minutes

Pax

3 pax

Tags: spicy seafood soup

Instructions

Step 1

Clean the fish and set aside.

Step 2

In a pot, add water, lemongrass, and asam keping. Bring to a boil.

Step 3

Add the sliced Bambangan to the pot and let it cook for about 5 minutes.

Step 4

Add the cleaned fish into the pot. Season with salt.

Step 5

Lower the heat and simmer for about 15-20 minutes until the fish is cooked through.

Step 6

Serve the Pinasakan Sada with white rice.

Ingredients

500g ikan basung (or any small fish like sardines), 1 Bambangan fruit (peeled and sliced), 2 stalks lemongrass (bruised), 3-4 pieces asam keping (tamarind slices), 500ml water, salt to taste
