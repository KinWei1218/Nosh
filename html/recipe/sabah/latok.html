Latok

Latok, or sea grapes, is a type of edible seaweed that is popular in Sabah, Malaysia. It is often enjoyed raw in a simple salad, allowing its natural briny flavor to shine. Latok has a unique texture, with tiny, green, bubble-like clusters that pop in your mouth. The seaweed is typically dressed with lime juice, soy sauce, and chili, creating a refreshing and tangy dish that highlights the natural flavors of the sea.

Cook time

10 minutes

Pax

3 pax

Tags: seafood

Instructions

Step 1

Rinse the latok under cold water.

Step 2

Mix lime juice, soy sauce, chili, and shallot in a bowl.

Step 3

Toss the latok with the dressing and serve fresh.

Ingredients

200g latok (sea grapes) 1 lime, juiced 2 tbsp soy sauce 1 red chili, chopped 1 shallot, thinly sliced
