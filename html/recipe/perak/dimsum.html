Dim Sum

Dim Sum refers to a variety of bite-sized dishes, typically served in small steamer baskets or on small plates. Originating from Cantonese cuisine, Dim Sum is often enjoyed during breakfast or brunch and is a staple of Chinese tea house culture. The dishes can range from savory dumplings filled with shrimp or pork to sweet buns and pastries. Each piece is a little culinary gem, crafted with care and bursting with flavor, making Dim Sum a delightful communal eating experience.

Cook time

30 minutes

Pax

5 pax

Tags: breakfast

Instructions

Step 1

IMix the minced meat, soy sauce, sesame oil, ginger, garlic, and vegetables together.

Step 2

Place a small amount of filling in the center of each wrapper, then fold and seal.

Step 3

Steam the dumplings for 10-12 minutes or until cooked through.

Ingredients

Dumpling wrappers 200g minced pork or shrimp 1 tsp soy sauce 1 tsp sesame oil 1 tsp ginger, minced 1 clove garlic, minced 1/2 cup finely chopped vegetables (e.g., carrots, mushrooms)
