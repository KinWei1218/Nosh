Rendang Tok Perak

Rendang Tok is the famous beef rendang that originated from the state of Perak, where I spent most of my childhood time before leaving for college. It is dry rendang believed created by the royal cooks, who had the luxury to access different spices that were out of the reach of the ordinary people in the yesteryears.

Cook time

50 minutes

Pax

2 pax

Tags: spicy meat

Instructions

Step 1

Blend all the spice paste ingredients until smooth.

Step 2

Heat the oil in a pot and fry the spice paste until fragrant and the oil separates.

Step 3

Add the beef cubes and cook until they are browned and coated with the spice paste.

Step 4

Pour in the coconut milk and water, then bring to a boil.

Step 5

Reduce the heat and simmer uncovered until the beef is tender, and the liquid has reduced to a thick gravy, stirring occasionally.

Step 6

Add the kerisik and continue to cook until the rendang is dry and the oil separates from the paste.

Step 7

Serve with rice or lemang.

Ingredients

1kg cubes of beef, 200ml thick coconut milk, 100ml water, 2 tablespoons kerisik (toasted coconut), 5 tablespoons cooking oil, 8 shallots (chopped), 5 cloves garlic (chopped), 3cm ginger (chopped), 3cm galangal (chopped), 2cm turmeric root, 5 soaked and seeded dried chilies, 1 teaspoon coriander seeds, 1 teaspoon cumin seeds, 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
