Gulai Tempoyak

Gulai Tempoyak is a bold and unique dish from Malaysia, combining the rich, creamy taste of durian with the spicy and tangy flavors of curry. Tempoyak is fermented durian, which gives the dish its distinctive sour and slightly pungent flavor. This curry is often made with fish, such as catfish, which absorbs the complex flavors of the broth. The dish is traditionally served with steamed rice and is a must-try for adventurous eaters and durian lovers.

Cook time

45 minutes

Pax

3 pax

Tags: seafood

Instructions

Step 1

In a pot, add water and bring it to a boil. Add the tempoyak, pounded chilies, sliced red chilies, turmeric powder, and lemongrass.

Step 2

Add the Ikan Patin pieces to the pot. Cook on medium heat until the fish is tender.

Step 3

Add salt and sugar to taste, then stir in the shredded turmeric leaves.

Step 4

Allow the dish to simmer for a few more minutes until the flavors meld together.

Step 5

Serve hot with steamed rice.

Ingredients

Dumpling wrappers 200g minced pork or shrimp 1 tsp soy sauce 1 tsp sesame oil 1 tsp ginger, minced 1 clove garlic, minced 1/2 cup finely chopped vegetables (e.g., carrots, mushrooms)
