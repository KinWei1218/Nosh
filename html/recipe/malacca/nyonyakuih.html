Nyonya Kuih

Kuih Talam is a popular Nyonya dessert known for its vibrant two-tone layers and rich coconut flavor. It's a traditional Nyonya sweet treat that combines a chewy, slightly sweet base layer with a fragrant green topping. The term "Nyonya" refers to the descendants of Chinese immigrants who settled in Malaysia and Singapore and adopted local customs and ingredients, resulting in unique fusion dishes.

Cook time

40 minutes

Pax

3 pax

Tags: desserts

Instructions

Step 1

Mix rice flour, coconut milk, water, and sugar in a bowl.

Step 2

Pour into a greased steaming tray and steam for about 15 minutes.

Step 3

Mix rice flour, coconut milk, water, pandan juice, and sugar.

Step 4

Pour over the base layer and steam for an additional 20 minutes.

Step 5

Let it cool before cutting into pieces.

Ingredients

Base Layer (1 cup rice flour, 1 cup coconut milk, 1 cup water, 2 tablespoons sugar), Green Layer (1 cup rice flour, 1 cup coconut milk, 1 cup water, 2 tablespoons pandan juice (blend pandan leaves with water and strain), 2 tablespoons sugar
