Nasi Ayam Melaka

Nasi Ayam Melaka, also known as Chicken Rice Balls, is a beloved dish from Melaka, Malaysia. This dish is a local variation of the popular Hainanese chicken rice. It features flavorful rice cooked in chicken broth and shaped into small, compact balls, paired with tender, poached chicken. The dish often includes a variety of dipping sauces and garnishes, making it a hearty and satisfying meal.

Cook time

1 hour

Pax

2 pax

Tags: rice meat

Instructions

Step 1

In a large pot, bring water to boil. Add the whole chicken and cook for about 30 minutes until fully cooked. Remove and let cool. Reserve the chicken broth for cooking the rice.

Step 2

Heat vegetable oil in a pan, saut√© garlic and ginger until fragrant. Add jasmine rice and stir for a couple of minutes.

Step 3

Pour in the reserved chicken broth and cook in a rice cooker or a pot until done.

Step 4

Once the rice is cooked, let it cool slightly. Form the rice into small, round balls.

Step 5

Shred the chicken and serve alongside the rice balls. Garnish with fresh coriander.

Ingredients

1 whole chicken (about 1.5 kg), 2 cups jasmine rice, 4 cups chicken broth, 3 cloves garlic (chopped), 1 thumb-sized ginger (chopped), 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, salt to taste, pepper to taste, fresh coriander for garnish
